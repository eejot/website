"Eejot" Means Light.

We are a US-based 501(c)(3) dedicated to lighting the spark of education in the remote villages of Nepal through computer literacy and open source technology.

The Story of Eejot

Founded by Prabhat Jha and his family, Eejot was born from a desire to give back to their home village of Sisautiya in the Sarlahi district of Nepal.


Sisautiya is a village where talent is abundant, but resources are scarce. Eejot is run solely by volunteers who believe that access to technology is the "light" that can guide rural students toward a brighter future in the global economy.

Our Initiatives

We don't just donate hardware; we build sustainable knowledge ecosystems using Open Source tools.

Computer Literacy

We establish training centers in villages like Sisautiya where students can learn basic computing, typing, and essential software skills.

Digital Library

Books are heavy and hard to transport. We focus on building digital libraries, providing thousands of educational resources on a single hard drive.

OSS Awareness

Commercial software is expensive. We promote Open Source Software (OSS) like Linux to ensure schools can maintain their systems without costly licenses.

Our Impact in Nepal

See the faces and places transformed by access to technology and education.

Empowering Youth: Providing the first exposure to computers for high school students in rural Sarlahi district.
Building Skills: Our computer labs offer practical training in essential applications, closing the digital divide.
Knowledge Access: Hundreds of gigabytes of world knowledge, available offline to remote communities.
